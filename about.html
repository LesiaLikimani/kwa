Kwa Short Background

Our History

KWA was founded to address the growing need for a sustainable water supply in Kerarapon Sub-location. What began as a community initiative around the Kerarapon Spring has evolved into a structured association with modern infrastructure, professional management, and a growing membership base. Over the years, we have expanded our sources, improved our distribution network, and strengthened our governance to meet the community’s increasing demand.

Our story

Inside Kwa

KWA is responsible for:

      ~ Supplying clean and safe piped water to members.
      ~ Raising and managing funds to grow water services.
      ~ Expanding water production, storage, and distribution infrastructure.
      ~ Ensuring members follow established rules and regulations for the benefit of all.
      ~ Designing operational and financial systems to ensure accountability and efficiency.
      ~ Developing, managing, and maintaining water sources, including springs and boreholes.
      ~ Protecting the Kerarapon Springs catchment area and promoting environmental conservation.

What We Do (Our Mandate)

      ~ General Manager The highest decision-making body of all members.
      ~ Executive Committee Chairman, Secretary, and Treasrer.
      ~ Management Committee Oversees day-to-day operations and strategic decisions.
      ~ Trustees Custodians of the association’s assets.
      ~ Staff Operational team managing daily service delivery.

Our Governance Structure

We are committed to providing reliable water services, protecting our environment, educating members on healthy living, and continuously improving infrastructure. KWA exists to serve the community—ensuring quality water, good governance, and sustainable growth.

Our Commitments to the Community

Over the years, KWA has modernized its metering system, expanded storage capacity, drilled new boreholes, reduced water losses, and played a key role in improving community infrastructure.
